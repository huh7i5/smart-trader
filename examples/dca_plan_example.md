# DCA Plan Example — $10,000 Starting Capital

## Plan Overview

| Parameter | Value |
|:---|:---|
| Starting Capital | $10,000 USDT |
| Monthly Addition | $1,000 USDT (from salary) |
| Investment Horizon | 12 months |
| Risk Profile | Balanced |
| Cash Reserve | 30% minimum |

---

## Asset Allocation

| Asset | Target % | Initial Amount | Monthly DCA |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| BTC | 30% | $3,000 | $300 |
| SOL | 10% | $1,000 | $100 |
| LINK | 5% | $500 | $50 |
| DOGE | 5% | $500 | $50 |
| GOOGLB (bStock) | 10% | $1,000 | $100 |
| NVDAB (bStock) | 5% | $500 | $50 |
| QQQ (bStock) | 5% | $500 | $50 |
| **Cash Reserve** | **30%** | **$3,000** | **$300** |

---

## Monthly Execution Schedule

| Week | Action | Amount |
|:---:|:---|:---:|
| Week 1 (1st-7th) | Buy BTC + SOL | $400 |
| Week 2 (8th-14th) | Buy LINK + DOGE | $100 |
| Week 3 (15th-21st) | Buy bStocks (GOOGLB, NVDAB, QQQ) | $200 |
| Week 4 (22nd-28th) | Replenish cash reserve | $300 |

**Important**: Active/booster buys require all three checks to pass. A separately configured baseline DCA may still create its small scheduled proposal, but it requires risk validation and explicit confirmation before execution.

## Rebalancing Rules

1. **Quarterly review** (every 3 months)
2. If any asset deviates >5% from target allocation, rebalance
3. Sell overweight positions, buy underweight ones
4. Always maintain 30% cash after rebalancing
