# Macro Event Calendar — Trading Guide

## Overview

Macro events are scheduled economic data releases and corporate events that cause significant market volatility. Knowing WHEN these events happen and HOW to trade around them is essential.

**Golden Rule**: Never enter new positions within 24 hours of a major macro event. Wait for the data, then act.

---

## Tier 1: Critical Events (Nuclear Impact)

These events can move BTC by 5-15% in a single day.

### US CPI (Consumer Price Index)

- **Schedule**: Monthly, typically 2nd or 3rd Tuesday at 8:30 AM ET (20:30 Beijing)
- **Why it matters**: CPI measures inflation. Higher inflation → Fed more likely to raise rates → bearish for risk assets. Lower inflation → rate cuts more likely → bullish.

| CPI Result | Market Reaction |
|:---|:---|
| Below expectations | 🟢 Rally (+3-10%) |
| In line with expectations | 😐 Neutral (±1%) |
| Above expectations | 🔴 Selloff (-3-15%) |

**Trading strategy**:
1. 24h before: Reduce speculative positions, increase cash to 40-50%
2. 0-15 min after release: DO NOT TRADE (extreme volatility)
3. 15-60 min after: Assess the data, run 3-Point Checklist
4. 1-24h after: Enter positions if checklist passes

### FOMC Rate Decision

- **Schedule**: 8 times per year, announced at 2:00 PM ET (2:00 AM Beijing next day)
- **Why it matters**: The Federal Reserve sets interest rates. Rate hikes are bearish; rate cuts are bullish.

**Key nuances**:
- The rate decision itself is often priced in
- The **press conference 30 minutes later** causes the real move
- **Forward guidance** (what they say about future rates) matters more than the current decision

### US Jobs Report (Non-Farm Payrolls)

- **Schedule**: First Friday of every month at 8:30 AM ET
- **Why it matters**: Strong jobs → Fed keeps rates high → bearish. Weak jobs → rate cuts → bullish.

## Tier 2: High-Impact Events

These can move BTC by 2-5% and individual stocks/tokens by 5-15%.

### Earnings Season (Big Tech)

Major tech companies report quarterly earnings. Since many bStocks (GOOGLB, NVDAB, etc.) directly track these stocks, earnings are critical.

**Key dates** (approximate, varies each quarter):
| Company | Typical Report Month | bStock |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Google (Alphabet) | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct | GOOGLB |
| Microsoft | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct | — |
| Apple | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct | — |
| NVIDIA | Feb, May, Aug, Nov | NVDAB |
| Meta | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct | — |
| Tesla | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct | — |

**Trading strategy for bStocks**:
1. 1 week before: Research consensus estimates
2. 24h before: Reduce position to 50% if holding
3. After earnings: If beat → add on confirmation; if miss → wait 2-3 days for dust to settle

### Major Semiconductor Events

| Event | Impact on DRAMB |
|:---|:---|
| Memory chip maker IPOs (e.g., CXMT) | 🔴 Increased competition, supply concerns |
| Chip shortage announcements | 🟢 Price increases, higher margins |
| US-China trade restrictions | 🔴 Supply chain disruption |
| AI infrastructure spending announcements | 🟢 Increased DRAM/memory demand |

## Tier 3: Medium-Impact Events

### Crypto-Specific Events

| Event | Impact | Typical Effect |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Bitcoin Halving | 🟢 Reduces new supply by 50% | Historically +100-500% over 12-18 months |
| ETF Approval/Rejection | 🟢/🔴 Opens institutional access | Can move BTC ±10-20% on announcement |
| Exchange Hacks | 🔴 Destroys confidence | -5-20% drop, usually temporary |
| Stablecoin Depegs | 🔴 Systemic risk | -10-30% if major stablecoin (USDT/USDC) |
| Protocol Upgrades | 🟢/🟡 Improves fundamentals | Usually priced in beforehand |
| Regulatory Announcements | 🔴/🟢 Depends on content | Can be very significant |

## Pre-Event Checklist

Before any Tier 1 or Tier 2 event:

- [ ] Increase cash reserve to 40-50%
- [ ] Reduce or close speculative positions
- [ ] Keep core positions (BTC, SOL) intact (Rule 1)
- [ ] Set alerts for the event time
- [ ] Prepare limit buy orders at support levels (do NOT place until after data)
- [ ] Clear your schedule for 30-60 minutes after the event

---

## Post-Event Decision Framework

After the event data is released:

```
1. Wait 15 minutes for initial volatility to settle
2. Read the actual data (not just headlines)
3. Run the 3-Point Checklist
4. If all 🟢 → Execute planned trades
5. If mixed → Wait another 2-4 hours for trend to establish
6. If all 🔴 → Hold cash, wait for better entry
```
