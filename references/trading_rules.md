# Trading Rules — The 6 Iron Rules of Crypto Smart Trader

## Overview

These rules are non-negotiable. They exist because every single one was learned through real losses. Breaking any rule — even once — can erase weeks of disciplined gains.

---

## Rule 1: Never Sell Core Positions in Panic

**The Rule**: If the investment thesis hasn't changed, the position stays. Period.

**Why it exists**: The bottom of every crash is where you feel the *most* scared. Your amygdala (fight-or-flight brain) screams "SELL EVERYTHING" at the exact moment you should be holding or buying more.

**Real-world example**:
- BTC dropped from $64,000 to $58,000 in one day (-9.4%)
- Retail panic sold at $58,000-$59,000
- Smart money bought the dip
- BTC recovered to $65,000 within 5 days
- Those who panic sold locked in a -9% loss. Those who held recovered fully.

**When CAN you sell?**
- The fundamental thesis has changed (e.g., exchange hack, regulatory ban)
- You need the money for a real-life emergency
- You've hit your pre-planned take-profit target

---

## Rule 2: Never Chase Pumps

**The Rule**: If an asset has risen >5% in the current day, do NOT buy it. Wait.

**Why it exists**: FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) is the #1 account killer. When you see green candles, your brain releases dopamine and tricks you into thinking "it's going to keep going up forever." It won't.

**The math**:
- Asset pumps +10% in one day → You buy at the top
- Next day it retraces -5% (normal consolidation)
- You're now down -5% and feeling terrible
- If you had waited 24-48 hours, you could have bought 5% cheaper

**Exception**: Right-side breakout confirmed by 3-Point Checklist (all 🟢). Even then, start with a small position (half the normal size).

---

## Rule 3: Wait for Confirmation on Data Releases

**The Rule**: Before CPI, FOMC, jobs data, or major earnings releases, place NO new orders. Wait for the data, then decide.

**Why it exists**: Trading before major data releases is gambling, not investing. The outcome is binary and unpredictable:
- CPI lower than expected → BTC can rally +5-10%
- CPI higher than expected → BTC can crash -5-15%

**The smart play**:
1. Close or reduce speculative positions 24h before the event
2. Keep core positions (Rule 1)
3. Hold cash ready to deploy after the data lands
4. Wait 15-30 minutes after release for the initial volatility to settle
5. Then make your move based on actual data, not speculation

---

## Rule 4: Cap Each Trade at 6-12% of Capital

**The Rule**: Never put more than 6-12% of your total account into a single trade. For a $1,000 account, that's $60-$120 maximum per trade.

**Why it exists**: Position sizing is the ONLY thing that separates surviving traders from blown-up accounts. Even the best analysis can be wrong, and when it is, small positions keep you alive to trade another day.

**The formula**:
```
Max Trade Size = Total Account × 0.10 (10%)
Example: $1,776 account × 0.10 = $177.60 max per trade
Conservative: $1,776 × 0.06 = $106.56 per trade
```

**Batch entry strategy**:
1. Identify target entry zone (e.g., BTC $58,000-$62,000)
2. Split total allocation into 3-5 equal tranches
3. Place ladder orders: $50 @ $62,000, $50 @ $60,000, $50 @ $58,000
4. This averages your entry across the range

## Rule 5: Always Maintain 30%+ Cash Reserve

**The Rule**: At least 30% of your total account must be in USDT (or stablecoin) at all times.

**Why it exists**: Cash is ammunition. Every major crash in crypto history has been a life-changing buying opportunity — but only for those who had cash to deploy.

**The math**:
| Total Account | Minimum Cash Reserve | Max Deployed |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| $1,000 | $300 | $700 |
| $5,000 | $1,500 | $3,500 |
| $10,000 | $3,000 | $7,000 |

**When to increase cash reserve above 30%**:
- Before major macro events (CPI, FOMC) → increase to 40-50%
- When Fear & Greed Index shows "Extreme Greed" → increase to 40%

## Rule 6: Never Override the Pre-Trade Checklist Verdict (One-Red-Stop Rule)

**The Rule**: If the 3-Point Pre-Trade Checklist returns a red warning (🔴 CAUTION) on either Smart Money or Retail Flow, do NOT buy or add positions under any circumstances. The verdict is final.

**Exception — Shield (Baseline DCA) mode only**: The weekly small-amount baseline DCA is exempt from this rule because its purpose is to guarantee minimum accumulation regardless of market conditions. This exemption applies ONLY to the Shield mode, never to the Booster (Spear) mode or any discretionary trade. See [baseline_booster_strategy.md](baseline_booster_strategy.md) for details.

**Why it exists**: Hype and good news (like earnings beats or CPI drops) create FOMO. Our emotional brains tell us "it's guaranteed to pump, let's bypass the checklist just this once." It is almost always a trap. Smart Money order book depth and actual taker flow show what big money is *doing*, while news only shows what they want you to *think*.

**The math**:
- A major company reports blowout earnings → Retail buys at the open due to hype.
- Smart Money check shows: `🔴 Sellers dominate (0.33x Bid/Ask ratio)`.
- If you override the rule and buy: You buy the peak and immediately face a -2% to -5% "Sell the News" dump as institutions distribute their bags to retail.
- If you obey the rule: You stay in cash and wait for the selling pressure to exhaust, saving capital.

## Common Psychological Traps

### 1. FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)
**Symptom**: "It's pumping! I need to buy NOW before it goes higher!"
**Cure**: If it pumped >5% today, it will pull back. Set a limit buy 3-5% below the current price and walk away.

### 2. Panic Selling
**Symptom**: "It's crashing! I need to sell EVERYTHING before it goes to zero!"
**Cure**: Check the 3-Point Checklist. If smart money is buying and the thesis hasn't changed, the crash is temporary. Hold.

### 3. Revenge Trading
**Symptom**: "I lost money on that trade. I'll make it back by going bigger on the next one."
**Cure**: After a loss, take a 24-hour break. Do NOT increase position size. The market doesn't owe you anything.

### 4. Overtrading
**Symptom**: "I'm bored, let me find something to trade."
**Cure**: The best trade is often no trade. If the 3-Point Checklist doesn't pass, sit on your hands. Cash earns zero but loses zero.

### 5. Anchoring Bias
**Symptom**: "BTC was $100K before, so $65K is cheap!"
**Cure**: Past prices are irrelevant. Focus on current fundamentals, fund flows, and the 3-Point Checklist.

## When to Break the Rules

**Never** — with one documented exception: the Shield (Baseline DCA) exemption to Rule 6, which exists solely to prevent zero-accumulation during prolonged bear markets. This exemption is explicitly codified in SKILL.md and baseline_booster_strategy.md, not a spontaneous override.

If you find yourself rationalizing why "this time is different" for any other rule, that's your emotional brain trying to override your rational brain. Follow the rules mechanically. That's the whole point.
